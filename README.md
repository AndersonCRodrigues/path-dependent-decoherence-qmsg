> **Scope note:** The decoherence model $\gamma = \alpha \cdot W_{\mathrm{bio}}$
> is phenomenological; this repository validates it operationally, via the
> bootstrap statistics above, rather than deriving it from first principles.
> A companion paper, *Geometric Response and State-Resolved Decoherence*
> (in preparation), derives the Born-Markov generator microscopically for
> an exactly solvable restricted case -- a commuting qubit-bath coupling --
> and tests directly whether the resulting purity-loss functional reduces
> to $W_{\mathrm{bio}}$. It does not, in general: the companion paper
> proves this explicitly, identifying two specific obstructions
> ($W_{\mathrm{bio}}$'s sensitivity to coherent dynamics that leaves purity
> unchanged, and a linear-versus-quadratic mismatch between the two
> functionals even in the purely dissipative case). Whether any restricted
> or reformulated version of this relationship can be established is left
> as an open problem there. The quantum-twin results above do not depend
> on the specific functional form of $\gamma$; they depend on
> trajectory-dependence being physically real, which the bootstrap
> statistics establish independently of this open question.
